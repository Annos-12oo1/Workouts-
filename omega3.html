Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats that support heart health, brain function, and overall wellness. They cannot be produced by the body and must be obtained through diet or supplements.

What is Omega-3?

Omega-3s are a group of polyunsaturated fatty acids, mainly including EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid), DHA (docosahexaenoic acid), and ALA (alpha-linolenic acid). EPA and DHA are primarily found in fish oil, while ALA is plant-based.

Benefits

  • Supports cardiovascular health
  • Improves brain function and mood
  • Reduces inflammation in joints and muscles
  • Promotes healthy vision and skin

How to Use

It’s commonly taken as fish oil capsules, with 250–500 mg of combined EPA and DHA per day recommended for general health. Always follow label directions or doctor’s advice.

Is It Safe?

Yes, Omega-3 supplements are safe for most people. High doses may thin the blood, so consult a healthcare provider if you take blood-thinners or have bleeding issues.
